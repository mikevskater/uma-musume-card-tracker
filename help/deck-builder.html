Deck Builder

Build and analyze support card decks for training. Add up to 6 cards (5 support + 1 friend), configure training parameters, and review detailed stat breakdowns to optimize your training strategy.

💡 Quick Start: Select a scenario, add cards to the 6 slots, then scroll down to see your training breakdown and deck effects summary.

Deck Slots

Adding Cards: Click an empty slot to open the card picker. Browse or search your collection and click a card to add it.
Removing Cards: Click the × button on a filled slot to remove a card. You can also click the slot to swap in a different card.
Friend Slot: The 6th slot is designated for a friend card. Friend-type cards can only go here, and non-friend cards cannot be placed in the friend slot. Friend cards appear at whichever training you assign them to.
💡 Limit Break: Cards use their current limit break level from your collection. If a card is unowned, it defaults to LB 4.

Training Controls

🎬 Scenario

Choose the training scenario. Each scenario has different base stat values and mechanics that affect training calculations.

👤 Character

Select a trainee character. This loads their base stats, growth rates, and aptitude grades into the training calculations.

🏟️ Facility Level

Set the facility level (1–5). Higher levels increase base stat gains and change failure rates for each training type.

😊 Mood

Set character mood. Very Good gives +20% stat gains, while Very Bad gives −20%. Normal is 0%.

🤝 Friendship Training

Toggle friendship training on/off. When active, cards with max friendship apply a bonus multiplier to training stat gains.

Training Breakdown

The training breakdown table shows calculated stat gains for each training type (Speed, Stamina, Power, Guts, Wisdom). Each row displays:

  • Stat Columns (Spd/Sta/Pow/Gut/Wis/SP): The calculated stat gain for that training, including card bonuses, mood, facility level, and friendship effects.
  • Energy: The stamina cost of performing that training. Negative values mean the training restores energy (common for Wisdom).
  • Fail %: The base failure rate at the current facility level when energy is ≥ 50.
  • Cards: Number of support cards present at that training. More cards generally means higher stat gains.
💡 Reading the Table: Higher stat values are better. Look for trainings where your deck's card bonuses concentrate the most gains. Green-highlighted values indicate the stat matching that training type.

Training Assignment

By default, each card appears at the training matching its type (e.g., Speed cards at Speed training). You can customize this:

Click a Training Name: Click any training row name in the breakdown table to open the assignment editor for that training.
Toggle Cards: Check or uncheck cards to control which ones are present at that training. This lets you simulate different card placement scenarios.
Friend Card: Friend-type cards don't have a fixed training. Use the assignment editor to place them where you want.

Deck Effects Summary

The Deck Effects Summary shows the combined effect values from all 6 cards in your deck. This includes:

  • Aggregated Effects: Total values for all shared effects like Training Effectiveness, Race Bonus, Friend Bonus, Energy Recovery, etc.
  • Per-Training Breakdown: How stat bonuses stack for each training type based on which cards are assigned there.
  • Race Bonus: The combined race bonus percentage from all cards — a key metric for competitive decks.

Character Info

When a character is selected, the Character Info section displays:

  • Base Stats: The character's starting stat values (Speed, Stamina, Power, Guts, Wisdom).
  • Growth Rates: Percentage multipliers that affect how much each stat grows during training. Higher growth = more stat gain per training.
  • Aptitude Grades: The character's aptitudes for surfaces (Turf/Dirt) and distances (Short/Mile/Medium/Long). Aptitude grades affect race performance.

Unique Effects

SSR cards have unique effects that unlock at specific limit break levels. The deck builder shows:

  • Unlock Level: The limit break level required to activate the unique effect.
  • Effect Description: What the unique effect does when active.
  • Active Status: Whether the effect is currently active based on the card's limit break level in your collection.
⚠️ Limit Break Matters: Unique effects only activate when the card reaches the required limit break level. A card at LB 2 will not have effects that require LB 3 or higher.

Deck Skills

The Deck Skills section lists all skills provided by the cards in your deck:

  • Hint Skills: Skills the card teaches as hints during training. Hint skills cost less SP to learn than their base cost.
  • Event Skills: Skills obtained through the card's training events.
  • Skill Cost: The SP cost to learn each skill (with hint discount applied where applicable).
💡 Hint Discount: When a card provides a skill as a hint, it costs less SP to learn. The discount depends on the card's hint effect level.
